# Getting Started { #getting-started }

Flash Me is a flashcard app for language learning. Create cards with words and phrases, organise them into sets, and study them in sessions that track your progress.

---

## Create an Account { #create-account }

1. Open the app — you'll land on the sign-in screen.
2. Tap **Create an account**.
3. Enter your **email address** and choose a **password**.
4. Tap **Create Account**.

You'll be signed in automatically and taken to your (empty) Sets screen.

---

## Sign In with Google { #sign-in-google }

Prefer to use your Google account instead?

1. On the sign-in screen, tap **Sign in with Google**.
2. Choose your Google account from the picker.
3. Flash Me creates your account automatically on the first sign-in — no separate registration step needed.

!!! tip
    You can use Google Sign-In even if you originally registered with email and password, as long as you use the same email address.

---

## Finding Your Way Around { #nav-overview }

After signing in you'll see five tabs at the bottom of the screen:

| Tab | Icon | What it's for |
|---|---|---|
| **Sets** | :material-library-books-outline: | Your card sets — the main starting point |
| **Cards** | :material-style-outline: | All your cards, across every set |
| **Study** | :material-school-outline: | Start or resume a study session |
| **Templates** | :material-copy-all-outline: | Reusable card structures |
| **Profile** | :material-account-circle-outline: | Your account and app settings |

---

## Create Your First Card { #first-card }

1. Tap the **Cards** tab (:material-style-outline:).
2. Tap the **+** button in the bottom-right corner.
3. Choose **Flash Card** from the menu that appears.
4. Enter a **word or phrase** in the *Primary Word* field (e.g. *hacer*) and its **translation** (e.g. *to do / to make*).
5. Tap **Save**.

That's the simplest card — just a word and its translation. You can add extra fields (fill-in-the-blank, multiple choice, extra notes) at any time. See [Cards](cards.md) for the full details.

---

## Create Your First Set { #first-set }

Cards are studied in sets, so let's make one and add your card to it.

1. Tap the **Sets** tab (:material-library-books-outline:).
2. Tap the **+** button.
3. Give your set a name (e.g. *Spanish Verbs*) and tap **Save**.
4. You'll land on the set's detail screen. Tap the **+** button to open the card picker.
5. Select your card from the list and tap **Add**.

Your set now shows one card. You can add as many cards as you like — including [Workbook Cards](cards.md#workbook-cards) for structured exercises.

---

## Start Studying { #start-studying }

1. Tap the **Study** tab (:material-school-outline:), then tap **Study a Set**.
2. Select your set from the list.
3. On the setup screen you can toggle **Shuffle** to randomise the card order. Tap **Start Session**.
4. The first card appears showing the primary word. **Tap the card** to reveal the translation.
5. Tap **More** to expand the full card, including any extra fields.
6. After reviewing the card, tap:
    - **Skip** — you knew it; the card is marked and you move on.
    - **Review** — needs more practice; you'll see it again.
7. When you've gone through all the cards, a **summary screen** shows your results for the session.

!!! tip
    You can pause at any time by tapping the back arrow. Your progress is saved automatically — tap **Resume** next time you open the same set.
